On the web today, vast amounts of information are being accessed by almost every person that has access to the internet. It is hard to imagine living in today’s age without internet access and access to information. Research papers, essays, and homework are made easier to do by the internet. Almost all resources are available online. One key aspect of this highway of information is a search engine. And the most popular search engine is Google. There is almost nothing you can’t search or learn with Google. It’s the easiest way to find information on the internet. Its intuitive algorithm helps pinpoint and narrow the search to make it accurate as much as possible and be readily available in a heartbeat. Google is not the only search engine available, but I think it is safe to say it is the most dominant search engine out there. But how did it become what Google is today?

1994

Birth of Yahoo!

Two electrical engineering students, Jerry Yang, and David Filo, at Stanford University in their quest to scour the web for information about their basketball fantasy draft have found themselves endlessly searching for information and suddenly had an idea to make it easier for themselves and organized the websites to make navigation easier. They created a website called “Jerry and David’s guide to the World Wide Web”. The site was a directory of other websites, organized in a hierarchy, as opposed to a searchable index of pages. And it was hit and used by millions of people. Their website then changed into Yahoo due to its characters being too long for an easily accessible domain.

In the mid-’90s, even though the popularity of the internet, its popularity did not generate income. When Mosaic was first launched, the first web browser, even though it had millions of users, there were no ways to generate income but charge users for its application. And this is where advertising came in. It ushered the way for small companies to generate income and grow to such an unprecedented rate.

1995

Yahoo decided then to launch advertisements on their websites and was completely worried, and afraid of alienating their loyal users. But, as time went on the number of users continued to grow which means more eyes on the advertisements posted on their website.

Excite

A company that rivaled yahoo at that time. A pure software company that is much superior to Yahoo’s website. Instead of Yahoo’s directories, Excite does the work for you- type in a query, the software does its job for you- it scours the web and presents all sites that contain the term that you entered. It is from what is a rudimentary version of a search engine that we know today

1997

As multitude of users continued to grow, companies like Yahoo and Excite reinvented themselves into portals - a one-stop destination packed with diversions from chat rooms, emails, stock tickers - to prevent users from leaving their site and to consume their advertisements. As a result, this created an extreme competition and resulted in extreme innovations, but it is unsustainable for some of the other companies who can’t keep up.

1998

While Yahoo, Excite, and other search engine companies kept raking in profits for their sites, they lost track of what they were initially doing: being a search engine.

Google was created as a research project by two Ph.D. students at Stanford University. Larry Page and Sergey Brin had an idea on how to make a better search engine that ranks results by counting the number of times the search terms appeared on the page. This algorithm was then called PageRank; it determined a website’s rank by the number of pages it is linked to and the original page will be the first link to be shown.

Deal of a lifetime?

At the time, Yahoo was dominating its other competitors, and its main rival is Excite. To help Excite fight back, a venture capitalist introduced Google to them and Google was hoping to be bought out. But Excite’s CEO was not impressed by Google’s program. It was not only Excite who did not see Google’s full potential but almost every other company in Silicon Valley.

Rise of Google

Determined to help fund Google, Page and Brin met with David Cheriton, a Stanford University computer science professor, and Sun Microsystems co-founder Andy Bechtolsheim, they have pitched their search engine and Bechtolsheim was amazed and wrote Google company a $100,000 check to kickstart their company. Other investors also contributed and made up one million dollars that helped start Google’s uprise. And by 1999, Google received a total amount of more than 20$ million dollars.

1999

As Google continues to establish itself, Bechtolsheim and its founders’ vision did not stop at scouring the internet in the U.S, but they wanted to extend their vision to make it global, accessible to everyone in the world. However, the same problem arises at Google: how do we make money? Should they follow the likes of Yahoo and other search engines alike with their advertising strategy, or to innovate a new way to advertise to generate revenue? Both original investors could not think of having Google be the likes of Yahoo.

Bill Gross is the founder of Idealab. He found a way to curate advertisements based on keywords. The idea behind Gross’s strategy is: whenever a user searches for or types a keyword in the search engine it will display a related advertisement to the page. This way advertisements will not look like a randomly generated advertisement and would not annoy users. Furthermore, these keywords are sold to companies so every time a user types the keyword companies who bought the keywords to advertise will show it to the page. Google found this idea and contacted Bill Gross to have a meeting on how Google would implement Gross’s idea to its own search engine.

Adwords

After numerous meetings between Google and Gross, Page and Brin walked away with no any kind of deal. And after a few months, Google released Adwords. Adwords is suspiciously similar to Gross’s idea. Gross took Google to court and he walked away with a settlement.

2004-Present

In 2004, Google went public but with a lot of doubt within wall street because of their unconventional way of selling their stocks. However, once they went public, their opening was a whopping success. Their stock price never went down below $100. With Google’s success they are still today one of the most dominant tech companies in the world.
