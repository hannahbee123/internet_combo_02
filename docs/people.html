Today, the internet is part of our daily lives. It became our routine to check our social media, watch videos online. As humans, we seek out control over something to feel important and this is what social media and other platforms give us. But how did these platforms come about in giving us this freedom to be ourselves, and communicate with our peers?

Media Revolution

In the mid-90s, the only way to consume music is by buying a record in a record store with the intent of listening to your favorite song. However, if you end up not liking the other songs part of that album, you do not have a choice to get a refund. But, during the media revolution, some tech geniuses found a way to revolutionize how we consume music.

1999

Napster

Shawn Fanning, the creator of Napster, had an idea to have a peer to peer system in downloading music because scouring the internet just to find one music file is too much work, but instead let users’ computers communicate with other computers to share their music files. And within a week thousands of users downloaded the Napster application for free.

Multiple concerns arise for Napster. Lawyers inside the music industry are increasingly aware of Napster and its implications that can harm the intellectual property of artists’ music being distributed on Napster.

2000

One famous drummer of the band Metallica, Lars Ulrich, confronted and went into Napster’s office with two lawyers to confront and ask Napster to ban users who distributed Metallica’s music within their application. However, due to this event, Napster just became much more popular with the public.

2001

During a long fight against a lawsuit that is filed against Napster violating the copyright law, Napster lost the fight. But this event will fuel the rise of revolution of what Napster was essentially about, giving people the power over corporations.

2004

When YouTube was founded in 2004 to offer service to the public that the public can share, view, create, content on their website. Youtube revolutionized the user-created content that echoed what Napster was essentially about: giving people the power to distribute their content. Because of companies like Youtube and other social media companies, it ushered in the era of what is called Web 2.0

Web 2.0

Web 2.0 is all about having the community be able to contribute and relate to the content that is being published on the web. Like Youtube or twitter, most of their content is created by the user. What made web 2.0 more apparent on the internet is the rise of social media. In the early 2000s websites like Friendster, MySpace, and Craigslist were some companies that offered users control over their content.

Facebook

Mark Zuckerberg, Facebook’s founder, had the ambition to create Facebook to be a tool to help communicate with people all over the world. And after a few years, almost everyone in the world is connected through Facebook.

Through Napster and other companies that revolutionized the way we use the web and its contribution to the society that we live in now, it’s undeniable that the power is in our hands. However, today, it can be hard to say that the power is in our hands because of all the information that these companies have on us.
