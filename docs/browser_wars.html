In today’s modern world, browsers like google chrome, firefox, and internet explorer are the most popular internet browsers there are. These browsers allow us to connect, browse and communicate with the internet. But in the early 90’s an unforeseen conflict between one of the biggest tech companies in the world and a group of computer engineers will take on the Goliath of the tech industry.

1993

Marc Andreessen

Andreessen had an idea to make web browsing more appealing and user friendly by adding images, audio, video capabilities. With the help of his college friends, they created the first graphical web browser: Mosaic.

In 1993, Andreessen and his friends posted Mosaic online. From there, it exponentially grew it’s users from twelve to million users in just nine months. Because of its growing popularity, the founder of Silicon Graphics: Jim Clarke.

1994

Jim Clarke

A newly resigned founder Jim Clarke helped further and funded Mosaic to make it available globally. Clarke saw the potential of Mosaic to be a business venture and possibly profit from it. However, there is a huge roadblock that they have to face: Microsoft.

At that time, Microsoft was the most dominant technology company in the computer industry, Microsoft has its own online system called “The Microsoft Network” or MSN. And in the middle of all of it is its founder: Bill Gates.

Bill Gates

A Harvard dropout who helped create Microsoft with the help of his friend Paul Allen. Gates’ vision is simple: is to make Microsoft software the sole program to be used in every office, business, and computer. And in 1993, about 90% of the world was running Microsoft windows on their computers.

Netscape

Jim Clarke’s newest company that is to rival Microsoft’s supremacy with the help of Andreessen and other computer engineers will soon launch it’s newest browser that will shake the tech industry: Navigator.

Launch of Navigator

At its launch, Netscape’s newest browser was the biggest and hottest topic in the tech industry. In just a month, they reached a million users. This news spread out like wildfire and Microsoft took notice. Microsoft, the most dominant company at that time, is tremendously worried about the rise of Netscape and its hottest application.

VS.

Declaration of War

As Netscape’s newest application grew unexpectedly, Bill Gates took notice and saw Netscape as a troublesome upcoming competitor and Gates wanted to keep his throne. He then sent out an email to his company employee to develop something that can rival Netscape’s newest application: Navigator. And this is the start of what we know today as “Browser Wars”.

Netscape continues to grow and was used by millions of people. The revenues keep coming in, but the troublesome Microsoft is preparing for war.

1995

Summer of 1995

Netscape hired an Antitrust lawyer to help them to fight Microsoft during negotiations with Microsoft. During the summer of 1995, a meeting was held between Netscape and Microsoft to negotiate, but what took place in that meeting was, till today, unknown. However, there are two sides of the story of what took place in the meeting. Microsoft’s side of the story was the meeting was as cordial and as casual as it can be, and on the other side of the aisle, Netscape’s claims were different.

Netscape describes it like a scene in Godfather - Microsoft made them an offer they cannot refuse- they offered Netscape one million dollars to have unlimited access to their technology. If Netscape’s story was true, it means they have broken the American Antitrust Law, this law constrains the behavior of companies that held a monopoly which Microsoft held due to their operating system: Microsoft Windows.

Going Public

Netscape’s rise into the computer industry did not stop them from going somewhere bigger. Jim Clarke urged Netscape to go public. And in its launch, they became the biggest and most fast growing IPO in the history of wall street. And from there, David becomes the Goliath. At their rise, Andreessen continued to pick fights with Microsoft by commenting on the public about his hate or dislike about Microsoft

All the comments made by Netscape against Microsoft their comments did not go to deaf ears and Microsoft started its envoy to destroy Netscape. After all, this is war.

Internet Explorer

In retaliation, Microsoft launched its own web browser: Internet Explorer. Internet Explorer’s team of engineers had some real advantages; vast financial resources, pools of intelligent talents, and their leader, Bill Gates, who is willing to do anything to maintain their power.

As Netscape and Microsoft engineers continued their fight for supremacy, Microsoft had its team of salesmen to negotiate under wraps to further the rise of Internet Explorer and sometimes negotiations that took place were borderline illegal. Jim Clarke claimed that Microsoft told Compaq, a pc manufacturer, that Microsoft will cancel their Windows license if they distribute Netscape’s browser.

1997

End of Browser Wars

Netscape’s downfall is that Microsoft is a billion dollar company and they can do a lot with it. As Netscape continued to charge its users for their browser, Microsoft launched Internet Browser version 4 and made it available for free and integrated into their windows operating system. As a result, Netscape had nowhere to go and at the end, Microsoft took the win and this marked the end of the browser wars.

As Microsoft celebrates its victory, they have found a new enemy: The United States government. The U.S government found Microsoft to have violated antitrust laws during their fight with Netscape and Bill Gates was interrogated and appeared in court.

2000

Result of the war

The federal judge in charge of the case against Microsoft found them guilty of taking advantage of their monopoly and violating antitrust laws. The federal judge suggested that Microsoft be broken up and as a result, their stock market prices plunged causing them to lose $30 billion dollars overnight. However, an appeal was made and found that the judgement is too harsh and rescinded its judgement to break up the company.

Netscape's Impact

Even though Netscape lost the browser wars, they did pioneer the rise of startups that will eventually take over the technology industry and will eventually give birth to google, a company that made Microsoft look old.
